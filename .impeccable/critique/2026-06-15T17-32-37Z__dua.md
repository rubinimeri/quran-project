## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** The page no longer reads as AI-generated in the damning sense. The most telling pre-ship fix was removing the eyebrow "Supplications" — that was exactly the small uppercase wide-tracked label-above-every-hero pattern the absolute bans call AI grammar. With it gone, the Arabic دعاء is the first word on the page: a deliberate, distinctive opener that no AI defaults to. The lantern-rise (blur→clear) hero animation is a specific motion material, not the generic fade-up applied to everything. The citation in Cormorant serif, normal-case, is correctly differentiated from the tab voice. The category-predictability caveat remains (gold-on-night for Islamic content is the expected palette), but this is the committed brand identity — identity-preservation wins here, it's not a defect.

**Deterministic scan:** `detect.mjs` returned `[]` — zero findings across all three files for this run. Clean.

**Visual overlays:** No browser automation available; assessment rests on source review and the deterministic scan.

## Overall Impression

The page has crossed from "solid foundation with clear issues" to "genuinely good." The hierarchy is now correct: the Arabic title opens the experience, the meaning leads on every card, phonetics are quieter, and the citation speaks in a different typographic voice. What remains are small-scope improvements rather than structural problems.

## What's Working

1. **The Arabic hero as first word.** Removing the eyebrow was the right call. The large دعاء resolving out of dusk via lantern-rise (blur+rise, 0.9s expo ease) IS the "Lantern at Dusk" north star. The supporting cluster (Du'ā + subtitle + divider) settling in at delay-400 gives the hero room to breathe first.

2. **Card hierarchy executes the Meaning-Leads Rule.** Arabic (visual centerpiece) → Translation (text-foreground/90, upright, most readable line) → Transliteration (gold/90, quieter) → Citation (Cormorant serif, normal-case — a different voice from the tab labels). A learner reads the meaning first. A reciter's eye goes to the Arabic first.

3. **Mobile scroll affordance is non-invasive and state-aware.** Right-fade appears on load (assumes overflow on mobile), disappears once the user scrolls to the final tab, invisible on desktop.

## Priority Issues

- **[P2] No recitation guidance on the card.** The card is silent on "when" and "how many times." Category name implies occasion but not count. Fix: add an `occasions` field to `Dua` type in `lib/duas.ts` (e.g., "3× upon waking") and render it as a small quiet line above the citation.

- **[P2] Tab touch target height below 44px guideline.** `py-2 text-xs` ≈ 28px height. Shared system issue. Fix: raise to `py-3` or add `min-h-11` on `TabsTrigger` in `components/ui/tabs.tsx`.

- **[P3] No deep-link to individual duas.** Users can't share or directly return to a specific dua. Fix: URL search param `?category=morning&id=dua-id` via `useSearchParams`.

- **[P3] No sticky tab bar during long category scrolls.** Tab bar scrolls off-screen on desktop when reading long lists. Fix: `position: sticky; top: 64px` on the tab wrapper.

## Persona Red Flags

**Layla (new Muslim / learner):** Translation is now the most readable line — improvement. But the card doesn't say "3× upon waking" or "after salah." She understands the meaning but can't determine the practice.

**Sam (accessibility-dependent):** No blocking issues. Keyboard navigation correct, reduced-motion fires correctly, lang/dir attributes present on Arabic text, aria-hidden on decorative fade overlay.

**Casey (distracted mobile user):** No major friction. Tab touch target height ~28px is below the guideline (see P2). iOS Safari viewport handled by min-h-dvh.

## Minor Observations

- text-pretty on translation reduces orphans on English meaning line.
- leading-none on Arabic h1 is correct for a single display word.
- mt-6 spacing after hero gives Cormorant name room beneath the large Arabic title.
- ❖ glyph at text-gold-muted/20 — intentionally faint, decorative, doesn't compete.
- Card hover:bg-card/60 deepens glass subtly for pointer users.

## Questions to Consider

- If "when and how many times to recite" were a first-class field, would the page feel like a practice guide rather than a collection? Is that better for the learner half, or does it add doctrinal weight Nur deliberately avoids?
- Could a sticky tab bar be implemented without reading as "sticky chrome" — can the gold underline and glass surface make it feel like part of the content?
- What would a brief category introduction sentence look like before the card list ("The Prophet ﷺ used to recite these...") — does it ground the learner or add noise?
